EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT
(Under Indian Labour Laws)

This Employment Agreement is entered into on [DATE] between TechCorp Solutions Private Limited, a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013 ("Company") and [EMPLOYEE NAME] ("Employee").

1. APPOINTMENT AND POSITION
The Company hereby employs the Employee as Senior Software Developer in the Technology Department at the Company's office in Bangalore, Karnataka, India.

2. TERMS OF EMPLOYMENT
2.1 The employment shall commence on [START DATE] and shall continue until terminated in accordance with the terms of this Agreement.
2.2 The Employee shall work Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (45 hours per week) as per the Karnataka Shops and Establishments Act, 1961.
2.3 Probation Period: The first 6 months shall constitute a probationary period during which either party may terminate this Agreement with 15 days' written notice.

3. REMUNERATION AND BENEFITS
3.1 Basic Salary: Rs. 8,00,000 per annum
3.2 House Rent Allowance: Rs. 3,20,000 per annum
3.3 Special Allowance: Rs. 1,80,000 per annum
3.4 Total Annual CTC: Rs. 12,00,000
3.5 Provident Fund: Company shall contribute 12% of basic salary to EPF as per Employees' Provident Fund Act, 1952
3.6 Employee State Insurance: As applicable under ESI Act, 1948

4. LEAVE ENTITLEMENTS
4.1 Annual Leave: 21 days per calendar year
4.2 Sick Leave: 12 days per calendar year as per Factories Act, 1948
4.3 Casual Leave: 12 days per calendar year
4.4 Maternity Leave: As per Maternity Benefit Act, 2017 (26 weeks)
4.5 Paternity Leave: 15 days as per company policy

5. DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
5.1 Develop and maintain software applications using Java and Python
5.2 Participate in code reviews and maintain coding standards
5.3 Collaborate with cross-functional teams
5.4 Maintain confidentiality of proprietary information
5.5 Comply with all company policies and Indian labour regulations

6. CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE
6.1 The Employee shall not disclose any confidential information, trade secrets, or proprietary data of the Company during or after employment.
6.2 This obligation shall survive termination for a period of 3 years.
6.3 Violation may result in legal action under the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
7.1 All work products, inventions, and intellectual property created during employment shall belong to the Company.
7.2 Employee agrees to assign all rights, title, and interest to the Company.
7.3 This clause is governed by the Copyright Act, 1957 and Patents Act, 1970.

8. TERMINATION
8.1 Notice Period: Either party may terminate this Agreement with 60 days' written notice after probation.
8.2 Summary Dismissal: Company may terminate immediately for misconduct, breach of confidentiality, or violation of company policies.
8.3 Resignation: Employee must provide 60 days' notice and complete all pending assignments.
8.4 Final Settlement: All dues shall be settled as per Payment of Wages Act, 1936.

9. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION
9.1 For 12 months post-termination, Employee shall not work for direct competitors in Bangalore region.
9.2 Employee shall not solicit Company clients or employees for 18 months.
9.3 Enforceability subject to Indian Contract Act, 1872 and judicial interpretation.

10. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
10.1 Any workplace issues shall first be addressed through internal grievance mechanism.
10.2 Sexual harassment complaints shall be handled as per POSH Act, 2013.
10.3 Labour disputes may be referred to Labour Commissioner, Karnataka.

11. COMPLIANCE WITH LABOUR LAWS
11.1 This Agreement is subject to:
- Industrial Disputes Act, 1947
- Minimum Wages Act, 1948
- Contract Labour Act, 1970
- Equal Remuneration Act, 1976
- Karnataka Labour Laws

12. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
This Agreement shall be governed by Indian laws and subject to exclusive jurisdiction of courts in Bangalore, Karnataka.

13. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
This constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.
